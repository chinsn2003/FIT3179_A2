Global Forest Change From 2010 to 2020

Overview of forest extent, spatial loss rates, temporal drivers, and impacts.

What Makes Up Earth’s Land?

Forest Composition

  • About 31% (~4 Gha) of the globe was covered by forest.
  • ~8.5% of that forest was classified as primary forest, ~20% was classified as planted forest, and the ~20% was classified as other tree cover.
  • From 2010 to 2020, global forest area declined by ~0.4%, indicating a slow but persistent net loss over the decade.

Overview of Tree Cover Loss

How is Our Primary Forest Changing from 2010 to 2020?

Annual Primary Forest Loss

  • Annual loss fluctuates between 3–4 Mha, peaking in 2016 (~6.1 Mha), remaining elevated in 2017 (~5.0 Mha), then easing to ~3.6–3.7 Mha in 2018–2019.

Remaining primary forest (% of 2010)

  • The share of primary forest remaining declines from ~100% in 2010 to ~96.3% in 2020—a reduction of about 3.7% over the decade.

Who’s losing, Who’s gaining?

Deforestation

  • Russia (~47 Mha) and Brazil (~33 Mha) lead; Canada and the United States follow.

Reforestation

  • Russia (~14Mha) and Canada (~7Mha) lead gross gains followed by the United States.

Net Loss

  • Russia and Brazil (~33MHa & ~29MHa) show by far the largest net tree-cover losses.

Net Gain

  • South Sudan (~0.59MHa) leads; Uruguay and Sudan show smaller gains.
  • Even the top net gains are well under 1 Mha, far below the largest net losses on the previous chart.

How Drivers Contributed to Tree-Cover Loss ?

Top Drivers

  • Permanent agriculture : Dominant driver (~6–10 Mha/year).
  • Wildfire & Logging: Strong surge in 2016, easing afterward.
  • Shifting cultivation: Moderate and relatively steady (~2–4 Mha/year).
  • Minor drivers: Settlements & infrastructure, Hard commodities, and Other Natural Disturbances contribute small shares (<~1 Mha each).

Who leads overall — and by driver?

  • Across most years, Russia and Brazil account for the largest share of global tree-cover loss.
  • However, the leaders shift when loss is broken down by specific drivers:
    • Permanent agriculture: Brazil
    • Wildfire: Russia, Canada
    • Logging: United States
    • Shifting cultivation: Democratic Republic of the Congo
    • Hard commodities: Indonesia, Russia
    • Settlements & infrastructure: United States
    • Other Natural Disturbances: Canada, Russia, Brazil, United States
